Space Encyclopedia

Stars are luminous spheres of plasma held together by gravity. Our nearest star is the Sun, providing energy that sustains life on Earth. Stars come in different colors indicating their temperature - from cool red stars to hot blue ones.

Planets are celestial bodies that orbit stars and have cleared their neighboring region of planetesimals. Our solar system has eight planets: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune.

Asteroids are rocky remnants left over from the early formation of our solar system. Most are found in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter, though some venture closer to Earth as near-Earth objects.
